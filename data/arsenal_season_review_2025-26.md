## 8. Web Presentation Design Ideas

The following are high-level design concepts for presenting this season review data on a web dashboard. No implementation code — just structural ideas.

### 8.1 Hero Section — Season at a Glance

A full-width banner at the top of the page displaying the most impactful numbers in large, bold typography:

- Three or four key metrics displayed as "stat cards": **W43 / D13 / L9**, **108 Goals**, **+63 GD**, **50.8% Clean Sheets**
- Each card could use a subtle icon (trophy, ball, shield, gloves) and a micro sparkline or trend indicator
- Background: a blurred hero image from a defining moment (e.g. Trossard's celebration at West Ham)

### 8.2 Form Timeline — Result Stripe

A horizontal strip of colored blocks, one per match, ordered chronologically:

- Green = Win, Yellow = Draw, Red = Loss
- Each block is clickable, expanding into a tooltip/popover showing opponent, score, date, competition
- Grouped or labeled by month with subtle dividers
- This gives an instant visual sense of form momentum — the all-green October block and the April dip would be immediately obvious

### 8.3 Competition Tabs

A tabbed or segmented control to switch between competitions (Premier League, Champions League, Cups). Each tab reveals:

- A mini league table or standings snapshot for that competition
- The match list table (as shown in this document) with color-coded results
- Competition-specific stats (e.g. UCL: "Unbeaten in 14 matches")

### 8.4 Interactive Scorers Visualization

A horizontal bar chart or "goal race" showing the top scorers:

- Bars fill up proportionally to goal count
- Hovering over a player's bar reveals their goal distribution: a mini dot-plot or heatmap on a pitch timeline (1'–90' x-axis, dots for each goal)
- Could include a toggle to filter by competition

Alternatively, a **radial/polar chart** where each concentric ring represents a player, and segments represent goals by minute range.

### 8.5 Monthly Performance — Area Chart

A stacked area chart or grouped bar chart by month:

- X-axis: months (Jul → May)
- Two series: Goals For (upward, filled blue/red) and Goals Against (downward, filled gray)
- Overlay a line for points earned per month
- Annotate the peak (October) and trough (April) with callout labels

### 8.6 Head-to-Head Grid

A grid or tile layout showing opponents:

- Each tile = one opponent
- Tile background color intensity reflects GD (darker green = more dominant)
- Show W-D-L record and score summary inside each tile
- Group tiles into "Dominant" / "Competitive" / "Struggles" categories

### 8.7 Late Goals — Dramatic Timeline
